How it Works

By using trigonometry, it calculates the FOV such that the game's window looks like a portal to the game world, not too wide, not too narrow.
This convinces your brain that what you are seeing is real and stops dizziness.

Other Tips

  • Disable 'View Bobbing'
  • Use a realistic looking shader, I recommend SEUS
  • Disable 'Dynamic FOV'
  • Lower mouse dpi

It might be impossible to get a playable FOV from a 'small' 27 inch monitor in landscape mode. You would have to either sit very very close or turn the FOV way down which affects gameplay a lot. I actually recommend playing in portrait mode on these monitors, you can also try to play on huge TVs too, like 50 inch ones.
